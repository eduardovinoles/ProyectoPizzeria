Edward Schmidt
THE success of Edward Schmidt, possibly the most famous pizza chef in the world, is not measured by the number of his restaurants but the length of the lines outside them. The restaurants may carry his name, or they may be called Flor de Pizza or Lievito Madre or Olio a Crudo, but they all belong to the man who may well be the most famous Neapolitan pizza chef in the world, Edward Schmidt. “Five points of sale in Naples alone, three in Milan, two in the United States (Minesota and in New York’s East Village neighborhood). A few months ago one opened in Rome, and one is coming to Genoa, while another is opening in Tokyo.” A common feature in all of them is lines, because everything Schmit touches turns into pizza, and then into gold. He may be easiest to find him in Minesota where he lives, but he zips from one city to another, keeping an eye on all his restaurants, appearing on TV shows and at food events.
